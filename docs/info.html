Carolina Trucking Academy

Our goal at Carolina Trucking Academy is to successfully place you with a trucking company prior to enrollment. Our career counselors will inform you of current openings and help you choose a career path that best fits your lifestyle. Once a pre-hire commitment is obtained, you are ready to begin your training.

FEATURES

Successful Job Placement

Carolina Trucking Academy has successfully placed 100% of our graduates seeking employment. We will continue to provide employment support after graduation, if needed. At Carolina Trucking Academy, we're here to assist you every step of the way.
Note: Unfortunately, we cannot guarantee employment.

CDL License Endorsements

All Carolina Trucking Academy graduates will receive a certificate upon graduation. Special recognition is given to the Honor Graduate of each class. Our instructors will also assist you in obtaining any special license endorsements you may need for your career in the trucking industry.

Successful Job Placement

We designed our training schedules to conveniently serve our students. Students have the option of attending four weeks of weekday training or sixteen weekends (Saturdays ony). Your training will prepare for you for a career in the transportation industry.

ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS

Age

Student must be 18 years of age. A student under the age of 21 cannot drive across state lines.

Drug Free

Must be physically qualified and drug free. A student must pass a DOT physical and drug screening upon enrollment.

Documents

Must have a valid North Carolina Driver's License or a Class A Learner's Permit. Must have a valid Social Security number.

MISCELLANEOUS

Students are selected on a first come, first served basis without regard to sex, race, creed, or national origin. We offer flexible class start dates and a low student-to-instructor ratio.

All Carolina Trucking Academy graduates will receive a certificate upon graduation. Special recognition is given to the Honor Graduate of each class.

The North Carolina Department of Motor Vehicles requires Carolina Trucking Academy to provide the student a minimum of 160 Hours of instruction before a student can receive a certificate of completion. Carolina Trucking Academy's curriculum exceeds the minimum hours required by NCDMV.

SCHEDULE INFORMATION

Week 1: Classroom

  • Orientation
  • DOT Physical
  • Drug Test

  • Log Books
  • Trip Planning
  • Brake Systems
  • Fuel Cards

  • Motor Carrier Safety Regulation

Week 2: Skills Training

  • 100 ft Straight-Line Backing
  • Pre-Trip Inspection
  • 45 Degree Backing
  • Coupling and Uncoupling
  • Sliding Tandems
  • Lock to Dock Simulation

Week 3-4

  • Two Lane Roads
  • Interstate Driving
  • City Driving
  • Night Driving
  • Fueling
  • Transmission
  • Double Clutching
  • Four Lane Roads
  • Hazmat

Weekend Classes: 16 Weeks; Sat. 7am-6pm

  • 2 Weekends: Classroom
  • 2 Weekends: Skill Training
  • 2 Weekends: Over the Road
